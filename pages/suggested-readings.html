Suggested readings

The following is a list of books, grouped by topic, that I have found useful while learning statistics and Python or that I find interesting and worth reading in general. As far as programming and statistics books are concerned, they are probably not the best out there, but as far as my personal experience goes, they are a good choice for beginners.

Statistics

  • An Introduction to Statistical Learning with Applications in R, by Gareth James, Daniela Witten, Trevor Hastie and Robert Tibshirani. I cannot emphasize how much I recommend this book. Clear, simple and concise explanation, easy to read and with explained examples of application in R. This book improved my knowledge of both basic statistical tools such as linear regression and more advanced methods such as PCA and Support Vector Machines. The book is freely available in PDF format at the author's website or at Amazon (not free here though).
  • An Introduction to Copulas by Roger B. Nelsen. Great introduction book to a more advanced statistical model as copulas. The explanation of the model is rigorous but quite understandable for everyone who is familiar with a bit of calculus. End of chapter exercises are feasible and useful. The price is a bit high, however if you are a university student there's a fair chance your university may have it since it is a very famous book on the subject. You can find it at Amazon.

Python programming

  • Think Python, by Allen B. Downey. Excellent introduction to programming and Python for beginners. It is free and a very nice quick start to Python. When doing these kind of books usually the authors need to make some choices and cure one topics more than the others. With this book I felt that an optimum trade off point has been reached and the reader can get not just a "quick-start" but a good quick-start. Highly recommend it. Get it here in PDF format for free or buy it on Amazon.
